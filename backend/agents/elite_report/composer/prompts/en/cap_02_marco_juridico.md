# Chapter 2 — Applicable legal framework

**Objective:** comprehensively map the normative framework under which the process unfolds. This chapter is referential: thematic chapters (4-9) invoke these articles when cross-referencing with empirical findings.

**Expected structure (## subsections):**

## 2.1 Constitutional framework

- Key electoral articles of the Constitution (for PER: Art. 2(17), Art. 31, Art. 35, Art. 176-187)
- Briefly interpret what each one establishes about the electoral process

## 2.2 Legal framework — specific regulations

- Electoral Organic Law (for PER: Law N° 26859) — key operational provisions (Arts. 190, 191, 272, 287-288, 343, 351, 358, 363, 380)
- Political Organizations Law (for PER: N° 28094) with amendments 31030 (parity) and 31170 (political harassment)
- Other specific rules relevant to the cycle

## 2.3 Relevant electoral jurisprudence

- JNE resolutions relevant to the cycle (e.g., Res. 0891-2025-JNE on electronic voting)
- Repeated doctrine on partial and total nullity (Const. Art. 184)

## 2.4 Applicable international framework

- Treaties ratified by the country: ICCPR Art. 25, ACHR Art. 23, other applicable
- Regional instruments: Inter-American Democratic Charter (Arts. 3, 4, 20)
- Applicable standards: OSCE/ODIHR, OAS/DECO, UN (2005 Declaration of Principles)

**Requirements:**

- Length: **600-800 words**.
- Every article cited must appear as `(Art. N of [Law])` or `(Art. N of the Constitution)`.
- Resolutions: `(Res. JNE XXXX-YYYY-JNE)`.
- Treaties: `(ICCPR, Art. 25)` or `(ACHR, Art. 23)`.
- Do NOT describe incidents of the current cycle (that goes in Ch. 4-9).
- Do NOT interpret compliance (that goes in Ch. 8 Breached rights).

**Tone:** legal-descriptive. Technical language but readable by non-lawyers.
