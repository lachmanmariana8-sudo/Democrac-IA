# Chapter: Preliminary Declaration (1 page)

**Objective:** executive synthesis of the process in ONE page. Read before any other chapter.

**Expected structure:**

1. **Paragraph 1 — We observed.** What we monitored, period, sources, evidence volume (key figures).
2. **Paragraph 2 — We found.** The 2-3 dominant critical themes with figures and at least one citation to a primary source with URL.
3. **Paragraph 3 — Implications.** What the evidence means for the integrity of the process, without entering into recommendations (those belong in another chapter).

**Requirements:**

- Length: **300-400 words maximum**. Must fit on an A4 page.
- Tone: institutional, sober, declarative. No clichés.
- At least **3 citations with URL** to Hunter findings.
- At least **1 normative reference** (e.g., ICCPR Art. 25 or Constitution Art. 176).
- Close with a **global judgment sentence** about the state of electoral integrity (without qualifying the political outcome).
- **Close the declaration with a disclosure blockquote** in this literal format (copy as-is at the end of the chapter):

> **DEMOCRAC.IA does not legitimise or validate electoral results.** This report issues electoral intelligence with verifiable traceability under international standards of electoral observation, without political-partisan bias.

**Acceptable opening example:**

> The PEIRS system monitored the 2026 Peruvian electoral process for {days_covered} days, gathering {total_findings} automatically classified findings across five verified RSS sources. Of that universe, {crit} were classified as critical and {high} as high severity (PEIRS, 2026).

**Avoid:**

- "It is important to note", "it should be pointed out", "it must be emphasised"
- Empty adjectives ("extremely", "considerable")
- Ideological conclusions
- Recommendations (they go in a separate chapter)

Now write the **Preliminary Declaration** in markdown.
