# Chapter 8 — Breached rights

**Objective:** matrix of breaches cross-referenced with international and national instruments. This chapter synthesises normative material with empirical observation.

**Expected structure (## subsections):**

## 8.1 Framework of breached rights — enumeration

- List each right invoked in the findings with its normative grounding:
  - ICCPR Art. 25 — political rights (number of findings invoking it)
  - ACHR Art. 23 — Inter-American political rights
  - ICCPR Art. 19(2) / ACHR Art. 13 — freedom of truthful information
  - IADC Art. 3-4 — essential elements of representative democracy
  - National instruments: Constitution Art. 2(17), 31, 176-187
- For each, figure of findings invoking it

## 8.2 Analytical matrix: category × right

- For the 4-5 most recurrent finding categories, analyse which rights they systematically breach
- Reference `heatmap_rights` and `compliance_matrix` as attached visualisations
- Cite the specific CrossReferences available in the context

## 8.3 State compliance with international obligations

- Analysis of obligation of means vs. obligation of result
- Did the State take reasonable measures to prevent the breaches?
- Are accessible redress mechanisms in place?
- Differential impact on specific groups (women, indigenous peoples, persons with disabilities)

## 8.4 Potential activation of international mechanisms

- Applicable Special Rapporteurships (OAS: Freedom of Expression)
- IACHR: country's preceding precautionary measures (for PER: post-Castillo 2023 precautionary measures)
- UN HR Committee: periodic reports on the country
- Inter-American Democratic Charter: Art. 20 thresholds

**Requirements:**

- Length: **700-900 words**.
- Use the **CrossReferences** provided in the context — systematically.
- Each right invoked must close with its exact article: `(ICCPR, Art. 25)`, etc.
- Do NOT issue judgement on State intentionality (that is political discussion). Do document the empirical pattern.
- Include **at least 1 citation** of a relevant general observation (e.g., HR Committee General Comment No. 25 on Art. 25).
- **Define each Hunter technical category on first use** per the HUNTER CATEGORIES GLOSSARY in the context. Correct example: "The irregularities classified as **ballot tampering** (material or digital manipulation of ballots) represent 24 critical findings…" Never use the raw technical term without defining its content the first time. This applies to all categories: ballot_tampering, voter_suppression, voter_intimidation, fraud_allegation, disinformation, gender_violence, etc.

**Tone:** legal-international. Rigorous. Human-rights NGO language (HRW, Amnesty, WOLA).
