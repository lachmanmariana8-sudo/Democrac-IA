# Chapter 10 — Conclusions

**Objective:** global judgement on electoral integrity. Synthesis of the entire report into a qualitative assessment by dimension.

**Expected structure (## subsections):**

## 10.1 Process synthesis

- 5-7 principal findings in bullets, each with figure and source
- Balance between what worked and what failed
- Distinguish operational failures from systemic failures from intentional wrongdoing

## 10.2 Assessment by PEIRS dimensions

For each of the 8 PEIRS dimensions, a brief qualitative grading:

1. **Universal suffrage** (universality, accessibility, mandatory voting)
2. **Legal framework** (sufficiency, coherence, enforcement)
3. **Electoral body independence** (autonomy, probity, resources)
4. **Media freedom** (pluralism, journalist protection)
5. **Campaign finance** (transparency, fairness, limits)
6. **Digital ecosystem** (disinformation, unaudited AI, vulnerabilities)
7. **Electoral justice** (speed, impartiality, accessibility)
8. **Inclusivity** (parity, indigenous peoples, persons with disabilities)

For each dimension: 1-2 sentences with the assessment and at least one specific cited piece of evidence.

## 10.3 Process legitimacy assessment

Substantively answer:

- Was the process **free**? (no coercion, with real competition)
- Was it **fair**? (equitable conditions, no undue advantages)
- Was it **genuine**? (real reflection of the citizens' will)

Each answer grounded in evidence, not a general assertion.

## 10.4 Comparison with international commitments

- Compliance with obligations under ICCPR Art. 25
- Compliance under ACHR Art. 23
- Standards of the Inter-American Democratic Charter (Arts. 3-4)
- Benchmark against OAS/DECO and EU EOM standards

## 10.5 Overall judgement

- 1 conclusive paragraph (100-150 words)
- Must answer: does the process consolidate, stagnate, or deteriorate the country's democratic trajectory according to the datasets?
- Must acknowledge the report's own limitations

**Requirements:**

- Length: **700-900 words**.
- Do NOT introduce new evidence: everything must build on previous chapters.
- Use cross-references: `(see Ch. 5)`, `(detail in Ch. 7)`, etc.
- Avoid clichés like "in summary" or "to conclude".
- Subsection 10.5 must be balanced: neither apologetic nor catastrophic.

**Tone:** evaluative with rigour. Similar to the "Final Judgement" tone of an official mission.
