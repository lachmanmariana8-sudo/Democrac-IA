## 12.4 Current regulatory framework — the existing gap

The country has no specific legal framework on AI in electoral processes. Identified implications:

1. **Absence of standards on explainability and algorithmic traceability** — there is no obligation to publish the model, training data, or performance metrics (false positives/negatives).

2. **Lack of right of appeal over automated decisions** — if AI classifies a tally sheet as "contested", there is no specific recursive procedure that questions the algorithmic decision itself.

3. **Gap in liability regime** — the criminal liability currently under investigation against officials is based on human omission (Art. 377 CP); there is no applicable framework for systemic errors of the model.

4. **Tension with the principle of tally publicity** (ICCPR Art. 25, ACHR Art. 23) — the UN HR Committee has held (General Comment 25, para. 25) that the count must be *transparent and the results made available to the public*. An unauditable AI system introduces techno-opacity prima facie incompatible with this obligation.

5. **Absence of certifying authority** — there is no body with specific competence to audit, certify, and authorise AI systems for electoral use. INDECOPI has no mandate; the APDP acts only on personal data.

6. **Right to truthful information (ICCPR Art. 19(2))** — citizen access to the code, training data, and validation reports is an instrumental condition of the right to receive information about the electoral process.

7. **Systemic confidence risk** — the combination of (a) AI implementation, (b) absence of public audit, (c) visible system failures, and (d) parallel logistical crisis generates a context of systemic risk to the legitimacy of official results.

## 12.5 Emerging international standards as reference

- **Council of Europe — Framework Convention on AI (2024)**: first international treaty on AI and human rights
- **UNESCO — Recommendation on the Ethics of AI (2021)**: principles of transparency, explainability, meaningful human oversight
- **International IDEA — Guidelines on electoral technology** (2019, updated 2023)
- **Council of Europe — Regulation on Machine-Assisted Voting Recording and Counting Systems** (CM/Rec(2017)5)

## 12.6 Specific recommendation to Congress

- Legislative initiative for an **AI in Electoral Processes Law** with mandatory minimum standards
- Applicable to the next elections (municipal/regional and following sub-national)
- Minimum framework: mandatory prior independent audit, publication of the model, right of appeal over automated decisions, certifying body with specific competence
