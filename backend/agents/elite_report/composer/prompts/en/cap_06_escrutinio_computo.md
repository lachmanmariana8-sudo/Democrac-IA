# Chapter 6 — Tally and count

**Objective:** analysis of polling-station tally, digitisation, validation, and centralised count.

**Expected structure (## subsections):**

## 6.1 Polling-station tally

- Tally pace and duration per polling station
- Extreme delay cases (e.g., Trujillo +24 hours for PER)
- Compliance with the public-tally principle (LOE Art. 287)
- Tally sheets: signature requirements and integrity (LOE Art. 288)

## 6.2 Contested tally sheets

- Figure of contested tally sheets in the cycle
- Most frequent causes (errors in totals, figures higher than eligible voters, signs of alteration)
- Geographic distribution
- Legal framework: LOE Art. 343

## 6.3 Digitisation and transmission

- STAE system in operation (for PER in Lima/Callao)
- Documented STAE failures (electricity, connectivity, lack of ink)
- Discrepancies between rehearsal and production
- Specific cases of ballot destruction due to technical failures (distinct from intentional manipulation)

## 6.4 AI-operator validation (SCE)

- Operation of the Electoral Tabulation System with AI
- Absence of certified public audit (structural issue)
- Comparative framework: Res. JNE 0891-2025 on VENP

## 6.5 Pace of the official count

- % of tally sheets processed within 24/48/72 hours after polling day
- Comparison with ONPE operational plan
- Transparency (public portal, external audits)
